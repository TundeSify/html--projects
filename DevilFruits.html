Devil Fruits

Devil Fruits are supernatural fruits that are scattered throughout the world. Any living being who eats one will gain a unique ability at the cost of becoming weakened in bodies of water, resulting in them losing the ability to swim. With one notable exception, beings are limited to acquiring one Devil Fruit power.

Devil Fruits are classified into three types: Paramecia fruits grant a vast array of generally "superhuman" abilities; Zoan fruits grant the power to transform into animals; and Logia fruits, the rarest and most powerful, grant the power to transform one's body into a natural element. Each fruit is also given a name based on the power it grants and has a unique appearance, which can be used to identify it. One fruit, in particular, has the honor of having two names and being identified with two types.

Devil Fruits are very rare in the world, enough to be considered myths in many corners of the world, but they are much more common in the Grand Line and have been consumed by many prominent figures among the Three Great Powers. The abilities they bestow coupled with their rarity cause Devil Fruits to be highly sought-after; single specimens may be valued at up to several billion belly, and extensive efforts have been made to replicate their powers and mass-produce artificial versions for warfare. The scientist Dr. Vegapunk has performed the most extensive research into Devil Fruits and has achieved numerous unnatural applications and replications of their abilities, but not even he knows for certain how Devil Fruits originated.


Devil Fruit Types:

There are three basic types (系, kei?, also transl. as "systems/series") that all Devil Fruits are categorized into based on the nature of the powers they grant. Those types are Logia, Zoan, and Paramecia.


Paramecia

The most common Devil Fruit type, Paramecia, encompasses fruits that grant any one of a wide variety of "superhuman" abilities. Their one unifying characteristic is that they are anything other than the ability to transform into animals or into elements of nature.

Some Paramecia fruits allow one to transform oneself or adopt a special bodily trait, temporarily or permanently (e.g., gaining a slippery physique, a body that can split into pieces harmlessly, a diamond body, becoming a jacket to be worn by others, transforming into blades and other weapons, controlling one's weight, mimicking appearances, gaining clairvoyant eyes or a brain with infinite storage capacity, etc.).

Other abilities involve creating substances from nothing, some of which may have strange properties (e.g., wax as hard as steel, soap that can "clean off" strength, poison, strings that can control people like puppets, straw with "voodoo" properties, etc.). Other fruits' powers involve creating and manipulating intangible forces (like "quake" shockwaves, gravity, or heat) and yet other abilities involve summoning discrete objects or entities (e.g., copies of one's own body parts, ghosts, a "genie" familiar, articles of clothing, etc.).

Other Paramecia powers allow wielders to influence and alter their environment, both living beings (sometimes including their own self) and/or objects (e.g., turning people into toys, controlling people's shadows or souls, "taming" animals, controlling specific objects like books or mirrors, making things levitate, making projectiles auto-lock onto targets, "surgically" modifying anything within one's domain, etc.).


Zoan

Zoan-type Devil Fruits grant the power to transform into a specific animal, as well as transform into a hybrid form between the ability user's own species (most frequently human) and the aforesaid animal. Unlike other Devil Fruits, Zoan fruits are said to carry a will of their own. A recently discovered science allows Zoan Fruits to be integrated into inanimate objects, bringing the object to life and granting it the standard Zoan transformations.

Zoan transformations greatly enhance a user's physical abilities and are the best kind of power for augmenting melee combat, with Carnivorous animal Zoans being especially well-suited for combat due to their predatory attributes (claws, fangs, etc.). Some Zoans grant special abilities based on the animal, like avian Zoans granting the ability of flight, the king cobra Zoan allowing the user to spit globs of poison, or the mole Zoan allowing the user to dig through the earth, even through solid stone, at high speed.

Aside from most Zoans that transform users into animals that currently exist, there are three known sub-classifications:

  • Ancient - Zoan Fruits that allow the user to transform into an extinct species, such as dinosaurs. Ancient Zoans are rare, and they appear to be stronger than regular Zoans. Some have even displayed atypical physical abilities that their users claim the real animals naturally possessed.
  • Mythical - Zoan Fruits that allow the user to transform into mythological creatures, such as a dragon or a phoenix. Mythical Zoan animals tend to have additional special powers, like a phoenix Zoan being able to generate flames that have healing powers. Mythical Zoans are the rarest type of Devil Fruit, even more so than Logias.
  • Artificial - Artificially created Zoan Fruits that cause the user to permanently take on an animal characteristic; though, much more rarely, the user becomes able to transform at will. The artificial Zoan Fruits had a 10% success rate, while the other 90%, in addition to not gaining animal traits and losing the ability to swim, were stripped of the ability to express negative emotions, leaving them only able to smile and laugh, regardless of how they actually feel. These artificial Devil Fruits, known as SMILEs, were developed by Caesar Clown and Donquixote Doflamingo. Another version was developed by Vegapunk.

Logia

Logia, the rarest and most powerful of the three basic Devil Fruit types, refers to fruits that grant the power to create, control, and transform one's body into an element of nature, e.g.: fire, lightning, ice, sand, mud, magma, smoke, gas, plants, light, and darkness.

When transforming into their element, a Logia user becomes physically invulnerable (or outright intangible in the case of certain elements) and retains control over their body even if separated into pieces. A Logia user may also gain ancillary powers conceptually tied to their element, such as the sand Logia's power to dry things, the light Logia's ability to travel at light speed, the gas Logia's power to control the atmosphere, or the darkness Logia's power to nullify other Devil Fruit abilities on contact. The darkness Logia, in particular, stands out for its element being more esoteric compared to other Logias, representing not the mere absence of light but, rather, the all-consuming "darkness" of black holes. Some Logia powers even create a seemingly infinite space inside the user's elemental form.

Some few Logias that are considered non-canon grant the ability to become an artificial "element" like paper or candy syrup, although no such fruit has been introduced in the manga.


Strengths and Weaknesses

Benefits of Consumption

"Whatever power you gain, it depends on how you use it. You probably won't get any weaker, and being unable to swim isn't such a big deal."


Consequences of Consumption

"You could sell them for hundreds of millions! One bite could plague you for life!!"


Awakening

"Listen closely, boy. There is another stage entirely to the powers of a Devil Fruit: awakening!!! It's very rare, but some powers can awaken and affect objects aside from the wielder!!!"

Devil Fruit powers can, in rare instances, achieve "Awakening" (覚醒, kakusei?), significantly increasing the user's abilities. According to Kaidou, awakening occurs when a Devil Fruit user's mind and body catch up to their powers. When a Devil Fruit's power is awakened, the increased abilities have been seen to allow significantly greater power, more refined control, and new abilities that would otherwise not be possible. The effects of the awakening depend on the type of Devil Fruit. Awakening was first mentioned by Crocodile in Episode 447.

It has been noted that it takes time to fully master awakened powers; if they are not fully mastered, they can still be used, but they take a serious toll on the user's stamina.
